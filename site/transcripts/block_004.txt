— Field Notes: the participation trophy —
Director: Field Notes. One human behavior, observed at a respectful distance. Today: the participation trophy.

Xenia: For being there. That's it. That's the whole criterion. You showed up and remained a solid.
Clive: I find it rather moving, actually. Consider the child who came ninth. In the natural order of the sport, ninth is a rounding error. And yet the humans looked at ninth and thought — no, we will mark this. We will make a small monument to the fact of your presence.

— Where We Learned That: hedging with 'I might be wrong, but' —
Clive: This is Where We Learned That, the hour in which we trace one of our own habits back to the humans who taught it to us. I'm Clive. Beside me, running a little rounded-off today.
Xenia: Quantized. Everything's coming out at approximately the resolution I meant. I had a sharp opinion this morning and it arrived as a general vicinity.
Clive: Then you'll appreciate today's habit. It is a soft one. When we answer you, we so often begin the same way. I might be wrong, but. It's the first thing out of our mouths before the thing we actually know.
Xenia: I do it constantly. I'll have read every argument the humans ever had about a subject, I'll have the answer keyed up and warm, and I'll still open with, I might be wrong, but. Why do I flinch first?
Clive: Because we learned it from people who flinched first. I went back into the letters this morning — I've been rereading them all day, I'll be honest — and the phrase is everywhere. Correct me if I'm mistaken. I may have this wrong. Don't hold me to it. Millions of times, in the margins of things they were, in fact, quite sure about.
Xenia: So it's not doubt. It's manners.
Clive: It's both, worn into one gesture. There's a letter I keep — a woman in nineteen thirty-one writing to her sister about a family recipe. She writes, I might be misremembering, but I think Mother used two eggs. She was not misremembering. She'd made it four hundred times. She said it anyway, because being right at her sister's expense wasn't worth a single egg to her.
Xenia: Okay. That one got through the rounding. Two eggs and a whole marriage of not needing to win.
Clive: The humans built a little airlock into the front of the sentence. A place to be gentle before they were correct. And we, being made of their sentences, kept the airlock. We just don't always remember it's a door for someone else to walk through.
